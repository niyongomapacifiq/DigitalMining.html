About The Project

Mining is one of the most important sectors contributing to Rwanda’s economy. However, mining workers face serious challenges such as tunnel collapses, toxic gas exposure, poor communication, and unsafe working environments.

The Digital Mining Safety and Monitoring System introduces modern technologies including worker tracking systems, emergency alerts, hazard reporting tools, and real-time monitoring dashboards to improve worker safety and operational efficiency.

Major Mining Problems in Rwanda

Low Extraction Recovery

Traditional artisanal mining tools recover only about 40% of valuable minerals, while the remaining minerals are lost in waste tailings.

Tunnel Collapses

Weak underground tunnels, poor ventilation, and lack of structural support create dangerous working conditions for miners.

Environmental Pollution

Mining runoff causes river pollution, soil degradation, flooding, and contamination of water resources in nearby communities.

Illegal Mining

Unauthorized mining activities lead to accidents, security problems, and financial losses for licensed mining operators.

High Energy Costs

Mining operations face expensive transportation and energy costs due to Rwanda being landlocked and having difficult terrain.

Lake Kivu Gas Risks

Methane extraction at Lake Kivu requires careful monitoring to avoid dangerous gas eruptions caused by destabilization of the lake layers.

Solutions

Rwanda Mines, Petroleum and Gas Board (RMB) and mining companies are adopting modern technologies to improve mining safety, environmental protection, and operational efficiency.

Mechanized Processing

Advanced shaking tables and magnetic separators help increase mineral recovery from 40% to 80%.

GIS & Drone Mapping

LiDAR drones and GIS systems help map underground tunnels and identify safer drill locations.

Digital Cadastre System

GIMCS technology helps track mining licenses, concessions, and real-time mining information.

Solar Mining Systems

Solar mini-grids and rechargeable LED headlamps reduce lighting costs and improve underground visibility.

Water Protection Systems

Automated retention dams and settling ponds help prevent mining wastewater pollution.

Gas Monitoring Technology

Real-time acoustic sensors and density monitoring systems help prevent dangerous gas eruptions at Lake Kivu.

Major Mining Hazards in Rwanda

Mining workers in Rwanda continue to face serious risks including tunnel collapses, toxic gas exposure, unsafe equipment, and dangerous underground conditions. This project aims to reduce those risks through digital safety technologies.

Gas Leaks

Underground toxic gases can cause breathing problems, suffocation, and sudden deaths in mining tunnels.

Structural Risks

Weak underground structures and poor support systems can lead to dangerous mine collapses.

Equipment Failure

Damaged machines and unsafe tools increase the risk of injuries and operational accidents.

Digital Mining Technologies

IoT Monitoring

Smart underground sensors monitor temperature, gas levels, and humidity in real time.

Worker Tracking

Digital tracking systems improve emergency response and worker accountability.

Emergency Alerts

Automated emergency alerts notify supervisors immediately when danger is detected.

Environmental Monitoring

Water quality and air pollution monitoring technologies help protect nearby communities.

Expected Impact

The system is expected to reduce mining accidents, improve worker safety, increase operational efficiency, and provide valuable safety data for mining management.
