    Using digital technology to improve mining safety, environmental protection, and operational efficiency within Rwanda's mining sector.

    Project Background

    Mining is one of Rwanda's most important economic sectors, contributing significantly to employment, exports, and national development. Despite this contribution, many mining operations still face serious safety, environmental, and operational challenges. Traditional artisanal mining methods often result in low mineral recovery rates, unsafe underground tunnels, poor ventilation, illegal mining activities, environmental degradation, and high energy costs. The Digital Mining Safety and Monitoring System was developed to address these challenges by introducing modern digital technologies capable of monitoring mining activities in real time, improving emergency response, supporting environmental protection, and helping mining companies make better operational decisions.

    Project Objectives

    Improve Safety

    Reduce accidents by detecting dangerous mining conditions before they become emergencies.

    Real-Time Monitoring

    Monitor underground workers, mining equipment, temperature, humidity and gas concentrations.

    Environmental Protection

    Reduce pollution by monitoring wastewater, tailings, and environmental hazards.

    Decision Support

    Provide accurate mining information to managers using dashboards and digital reports.

    Current Challenges in Rwanda's Mining Industry

    Low Extraction Recovery

    Many artisanal mining sites recover only about 40% of valuable minerals, leaving significant quantities in tailings.

    Unsafe Underground Tunnels

    Poor tunnel design, weak structural supports, and limited ventilation increase accident risks.

    Environmental Pollution

    Mining runoff pollutes rivers, damages ecosystems, and affects nearby communities.

    Illegal Mining

    Unauthorized mining causes revenue losses, security concerns, and fatal accidents.

    High Energy Costs

    Deep-rock mining requires expensive equipment and reliable energy sources.

    Lake Kivu Gas Risks

    Methane extraction requires continuous monitoring to prevent dangerous gas eruptions.

    Technologies Used

    Internet of Things (IoT)

    Real-time environmental monitoring using smart sensors.

    GIS Mapping

    Digital maps improve mine planning and safety.

    Drones & LiDAR

    Inspect hazardous areas without exposing workers to danger.

    Cloud Database

    Store mining reports securely and make them available instantly.

    Artificial Intelligence

    Predict possible mining hazards using historical safety data.

    Solar Energy

    Power underground monitoring equipment sustainably.

    Expected Impact

    Our system aims to reduce workplace accidents, increase mineral recovery, improve environmental management, strengthen emergency response, and support Rwanda's vision of a safer and technology-driven mining industry.

    Our Mission

    Our Vision
